Research Identity

I sit at the intersection of AI education, health communication, and behavioral science. My primary research asks how AI–human collaboration transforms learning — not as a tool question, but as a fundamental shift in how knowledge is created.

I call my approach "green apple logic" — designing environments where healthy choices feel natural rather than prescribed. Drawing from Yeager's wise interventions, Crum's mindset science, Falk's neural self-relevance work, and Mollick's co-intelligence framework, I design interventions that honor the integration of physical and psychological wellbeing across cultural contexts.

Background

Bilingual in English and Korean, with a multicultural background spanning multiple countries. Certified Pilates instructor and leader of a Saturday reading group on mind-body connection. This embodied practice shapes how I think about intervention design, learning environments, and the integration of cognitive and somatic experience.
