What You Need to Know This Month

Summer Internship Offer? Secure Your Work Authorization First

If you have an internship lined up for this summer, your next step is employment authorization — do not begin work without it.

• F-1 students: Apply for Curricular Practical Training (CPT) through myIO at least three weeks before your start date. You will need a course registration and an employer verification form.
• J-1 students: Apply for Academic Training (AT) through myIO at least three weeks before your start date.
• Important: Starting work before you receive your updated I-20 (F-1) or DS-2019 (J-1) can result in termination of your visa status.

Tax Season: What International Students Need to File

Three key facts:

1. All international students classified as nonresidents for tax purposes must file Form 8843 annually — even with zero U.S. income. Deadline: June 15.
2. Students who earned U.S.-based income (salary, wages, scholarships, grants) must file additional forms by April 15.
3. UT Austin provides free access to Sprintax for one federal tax return. Visit the ISSS Tax Information page to access your code.

Spring Break: Reminders Before You Travel

• Traveling internationally? Request a travel signature on your I-20 or DS-2019 through myIO before you leave. Without a valid signature, re-entry to the U.S. may be delayed or denied.
• Re-entering the U.S.? Carry your valid passport, visa stamp, and signed I-20 or DS-2019.
• Staying in Austin? This is a good window to get ahead on CPT applications or tax filing.
