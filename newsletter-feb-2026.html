Why This Matters

Starting in a new country, lab, or program often comes with a quiet question:

“Do I really belong here?”

Dr. Gregory Walton from Stanford calls this belonging uncertainty — the tendency to interpret everyday challenges as signs that we don’t fit in. It’s common. And it’s temporary.

The good news: research shows that when we support others, we strengthen our own sense of belonging.

What We’ll Do

✍ Workshop: Design Your Belonging at UT — A practical framework to reframe transition challenges.
Takeaways
• Understand the psychology of belonging uncertainty.
• Learn a practical framework to reframe transition challenges.

Belonging creates psychological safety. And psychological safety unlocks confidence, clarity, and connection.
