The Gap

Same product page. Different purchase logic.

Korean copy sells through mood and texture—“a mermaid’s magic.” NA shoppers scan for shade count, wear time, and whether it transfers. The gap wasn’t language. It was how each audience decides to buy.

Deliverable 1

18 product pages, rewritten for purchase confidence.

I benchmarked Sephora, Rhode, and Laneige NA to understand what copy patterns drive conversion. Then I rewrote every line to lead with function—shade count, wear time, finish, transfer—instead of mood.

Deliverable 2

Staff training system—one URL, zero onboarding friction.

The Seongsu flagship staff relied on KakaoTalk images for training—scattered, hard to find, impossible to update. I built a mobile-first web guide with 11 bilingual customer scenarios, shift checklists, and store procedures.

Reflection

What this project taught me.

Good localization isn’t translation. It’s understanding that the same product solves different problems for different people—and designing the experience around their decision logic, not yours.

The retail floor taught me something I couldn’t learn from data alone: hesitation has a body language. The moment a shopper tilts a product back toward the shelf is the moment your copy failed. That observation shaped every line I wrote.
