The Gap

Same product page. Different purchase logic.

Korean copy sells through mood and texture — “a mermaid’s magic.” NA shoppers scan for shade count, wear time, and whether it transfers. The gap wasn’t language. It was how each audience decides to buy.

Reflection

What this project taught me.

Good localization isn’t translation. It’s understanding that the same product solves different problems for different people — and designing the experience around their decision logic, not yours.

“The moment a shopper tilts a product back toward the shelf is the moment your copy failed.”

The retail floor taught me something I couldn’t learn from data alone: hesitation has a body language. That observation shaped every line I wrote — and changed how I think about communication design.
